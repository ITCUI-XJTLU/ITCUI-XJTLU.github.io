1. Problem Defination

Medical code predict is the task of transforming medical information in a patient’s health records into structured codes so that they can be used for statistical analysis.

On the one hand, the clinical notes is an essential part of patients’ medical information and provide important textual data for intelligence healthcare application. However, conventional process of medical codes prediction is costly , time-consuming and error-proning. An expert clinical coder is expected to decipher a large number of documents about a patient’s episode of care, and to select the most accurate codes from a large classification system (or an ontology), according to the contexts in the various documents and the regularly updated coding guidelines.

On the other hand, in recent years, pre-trained language models, such as BERT and its varieties, develop rapidly and dominates many challenging tasks in NLP.

Therefore, we hope to use BERT and its varieties to do the medical code preidction task and improve thier performence as much as possible. And because the limit of computation resource, we choose the top-50 codes to predict. You could change my codes predict all medical codes easily .

3. Related Work and Challenges

Medical code prediction is not a new topic. Perotte et.al.,2014 proposed an SVM-based classification algorithm. With the dramatical development of deep-learning and nlp, convolutional neural network are now the most coommon architecture. Mullenbach et.al.,2018 proposed CAML , which is a CNN-based label-wise attention model. Liet.al. proposed MultiResCNN , which add residual network into the model.

The Challenging of our poject is that transformer models, such as BERT and Clinical-BERT, cannot read long text (more than 512 tokens) and clinical notes are usually over 1000 words. Although BERT and its varieties performs pretty well on many tasks, they cannot beat CNN-based models. Because of the self-attention mechanism and limited computation resource, most BERT models and its varieties can take at most 512 words at one time. But, here is the distribution of text length of MIMIC III dataset :

We could seen that a large percentage of data have more than 512 words. It is reasonable that many important information for predicting medical codes are contained behind 512 words and BERT model may just omit the valuable information. Therefore, the goal of our project is to overcome this difficaluty . We hope to let the BERT models have access to more useful information without increasing our computation recource.

4.4 MultiBERT + Data Augmentation

Apart from changing the design of models, we could also do the data augmentation to improve the performence. In fact, due to the limit of computational rescource, we could at most use 3 different BERT models to take the input text, which means that the heirarchical structure discussed in 4.2 and 4.3 could only read about 512*3 = 1536 words. But, due to the rule of tokenization of BERT , one word may be splited into different parts for models, the numble of word our heirarchical model could deal with would decrease to about 1200 words. From the distribution of the length of input text in 3.0 , it seems that our model could still not make full use of data . So, we implement the following data augmentation:

  • In trainning process, if the length of input text is larger than 1800, we use an extractive text summarization model (roberta-mo from transformersum) to reduce the length of text about 1200
  • In trainning process , if the length of input text is larger than 1600 , we split the text into multiple text chunks , so that each of the length of text chunk is about 1000 words . If the length of some text chunk is has to below 800 , we use the raw text to complement the text chunks , so that each of text chunks has about 1000 words .
  • In testing process, if the length of text input is larger than 1600, we also use extractive text summarization model to reduce the input . In the following, we will compare the performence of models by different test input text . (Thne original one is just to truncate the input when the length is over 1600 , the newer one is to summarize the input text .) Since we do not sure whether the summarization of test data could improve the performence , we do use both of data (raw text data and summarized data, which will be called DA1 and DA2 in the following part ) .

6. Future Work:

In fact , as many papers mentioned , compared with CNN-based models , BERT and its varieties does notperform very well in medical code prediction . Improving the performence further is difficult , and we have to notice that the computation resource of BERT to do this terrible . Based on this recent review of medical code prediction , here are some advice for future compromising direction :

  • Understanding the data better . Clinical notes is ususlly very long , we try to use some develpoed model to summariza them . But this is not enough . IF hoping to enhance the performence more, we have to know more about the data , how are the clinical notes made ? what parts does cinical notes have ? Which part is usually import for the prediction ?
  • Coding from heterogeneous, incomplete, and noisy sources . In this project , we only use the notetable oof MIMIC III dataset , but the most clinical data in MIMIC III dataset is numerical data , such as results of different experience of patients . Combining those data with discharged summary is promising . But we have to mention that the way of combinging data is difficult.
